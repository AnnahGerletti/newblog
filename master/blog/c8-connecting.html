Have you tried using any of the insight and techniques for growing trust and sincerity? How did it go?

Trust is very important in all relationships work and personal. It is also very difficult to earn, but once earned the relationship can grow and is much stronger.

There are three things I do to build trust, first I listen, second I ask questions and third I provide feedback. I feel I am a good listener and this helps me to build trust within my relationship. Listening is an important technique for establishing trust because people respond positively when they feel they have been heard. Asking probing and open ended questions will also show that you were listening and you are empathetic to their situation. Lastly providing feedback is very important.

The political awareness exercise:

The situation I have chosen to discuss is something that started happening to me when I moved to New Zealand. It may seem silly to some and not important to others but it still caused me grief. I am talking about the use of the word “try” or "taste" at a bar.

These words mean two very different things when in USA vs. New Zealand. Sitting at a local Wellington pub with your mates and you ask to "try" a beer to the Kiwi bartender you have just told them you are committed to drinking a full pint, and this is what you get. When really all you wanted was to have a little sip to decide if it was right for you. Over in the states if you say to an American bartender you would like to "try" a beer, they provide you with a small portion to try.

The interesting thing is I can totally understand how it sounds to a bartender. The use of the verb try means to “give it a go” but to a Kiwi that seems to mean give it a go in a full glass. While the word "taste" is really saying you would like to have a small portion of the beer before you commit.

Either way I think when moving to a new country and in all situations it is important to think about how words and different sayings can be interpreted from the other side of the conversation. I need to really focus on which word I use when I am at the bar ordering a beer.
